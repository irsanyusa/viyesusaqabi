12 Best Cotton Sheets of 2024

Posted by Martina Birk on Thursday, June 13, 2024

Maybe you’re tired of tossing and turning each night, or maybe you’ve found the best mattress and are excited to complete your bedroom upgrade. Either way, you know that while a good set of sheets can take sleep from passable to pleasant, the best sheets will help make every sleep a great one.

Of course, “best” is subjective and everyone is looking for something a little bit different. With so many brands and styles of bedding to choose from, it can be overwhelming to know where to start—should you be looking at bamboo sheets, or searching for the best linen bed sheets? If in doubt, we suggest you choose cotton.

As a natural fiber, cotton weaves into breathable fabrics that are soft on the skin while still retaining a crispness. High-quality cotton sheets are known for their durability and should last you a long time, even when used on a nightly basis. Not only that, they come in several variations, from percale, which has the feel of a freshly ironed dress shirt, to sateen, which achieves a smoother, satiny feel. Each weave has its benefits, so you’re sure to find the right fit for your bed.

To help you determine what that is, we’ve put together this guide on the best cotton sheets that will ensure a dreamy night’s sleep.

What to consider when buying cotton sheets

Are you a hot or cold sleeper?

Some people sleep “hot” and want bedding that will keep them cool and ventilated at night, while others want insulation and warmth. Even the most high quality sheets won’t feel comfortable if they’re not designed to regulate temperature in the way you need. By understanding which kind of sleeper you are, you can better identify which kind of sheets you need. Cotton can work for both kinds, depending on the weave: Cotton percale is looser, crisper, and cooler, while cotton sateen will be softer and cozier. Tip: Believe it or not, you can buy temperature-regulating bedding. We’ve rounded up the best cooling sheets, for those who run hot.

How much do you want to spend?

This list includes sheet sets that range from approximately $40 to $400, with many products somewhere in between. A lower budget doesn’t need to mean poor quality sheets, but it will impact which options are worth exploring. In general, high quality fibers command a higher price, but every product on this list is made from 100% cotton, so you can shop with confidence.

Do you want a top sheet?

Top sheets are no longer as ubiquitous as they once were and this is reflected in many of the sheet sets that are being sold today. While some still include a fitted sheet, top sheet, and one or two pillow cases, a growing number of sets have omitted the top sheet as customers increasingly decide they no longer require them. This isn’t necessarily a bad thing; if you don’t want a top sheet, you’ll be saving money by purchasing a set that doesn’t include one by default. However, if you do prefer to make your bed with a top sheet, be sure to verify if the basic set includes one, as this could otherwise result in a bad surprise after checkout.

Frequently asked questions (FAQs)

What's the best thread count for cotton sheets?

Thread count is an important metric, but not the only one to consider when assessing sheets, since a higher count is not always a better quality product. Thread count refers to the number of threads in a single square inch of fabric and therefore the tightness of the weave: A high thread count means a tighter weave that will be smoother and more insulating than a fabric with lower thread count.

As a general rule, look for cotton sheets with a thread count between 200 and 600. More specifically, cotton percale should be between 200-400 and cotton sateen should be 300-600. This will ensure a sufficient weave to create either cooling or silky effects, without any artificial inflation (which is usually achieved by splitting fibers into cheaper, thinner threads). Our guide to the best thread count for sheets goes into more detail about this hot button issue in the bedding realm.

Is it better to have 100% cotton sheets?

Cotton is a natural fiber and is prized for its breathability, durability, and natural softness, which is why it makes such good bedding. As such, when a material is 100% cotton, you can be confident in the quality of the fabric you’re buying. Less than 100% means that the fabric is made from a cotton blend, usually polyester, which could make the sheets more affordable, but will likely result in less breathability and comfort on the skin than 100% cotton.

Depending on your priorities, it may not be critical to have 100% cotton, but we would recommend it as the best quality for cotton bed sheets. If you’re still not sold on cotton, you could try something like bamboo—we’ve compiled our picks for the best bamboo sheets here.

What is the difference between 100% cotton and 100% cotton percale?

When something is listed as 100% cotton, it means that it is made solely from cotton fibers that may be woven in any style. Percale refers to a specific weave of cotton, not a fiber. Therefore, when something is described as “100% cotton percale,” it means that it is made solely from cotton fibers that have then been used in a percale weave.

Percale is a one-over-one-under style of weave, as opposed to sateen’s three-over-one-under weave, and this results in a lightweight, cooling fabric that is well-suited for hot sleepers. This is why some brands like to highlight that their fabric has a percale weave.

What are the softest sheets that don't pill?

As a general rule, pilling is more likely with lower quality fibers. Therefore, look for sheets made from fibers like 100% Egyptian cotton or 100% Pima cotton to be more confident in their pill-resistance. Cotton sateen is a softer finish than percale, so also be sure to opt for sheets with that weave. However, a lot of this will also depend on how well you take care of your sheets, as all cotton is susceptible to pilling over time. Make sure to read the care instructions for your specific purchase and only machine wash and dry at the appropriate settings. For more on this, check out our guide on how often you should wash your sheets.

What kind of cotton are hotel sheets?

Hotels have to accommodate a wide range of sleepers, so they usually have to pick a more middle-ground, high-quality option that will satisfy the largest number of guests. They are also more focused on aesthetics, so they are likely to prioritize sheets with a smooth drape and a crisp finish. As such, many hotels opt for cotton percale made from high quality fibers, that are smooth and comfortable, but also cooling.
